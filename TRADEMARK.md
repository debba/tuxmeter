## What You Can Do

- Use "Tuxmeter" to refer to this project in articles, blog posts, and discussions
- Say your project is "compatible with Tuxmeter" or "built for Tuxmeter"
- Use the name in community contexts when referring to the official project

## What You Cannot Do

- Use "Tuxmeter" as the name of a fork or derivative work
- Use the Tuxmeter logo in your own project, product, or service
- Imply that your project is endorsed by, affiliated with, or an official part of Tuxmeter
- Register domain names or social media accounts using "Tuxmeter"

## Forks

If you fork Tuxmeter, you must:

1. Choose a different name for your fork
2. Remove the Tuxmeter logo and branding
3. Clearly state that your project is a fork and not the official Tuxmeter
